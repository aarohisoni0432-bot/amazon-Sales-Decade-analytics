Amazon India – A Decade of Sales Analytics (2015–2025)

End-to-end analytics pipeline: data cleaning, SQL analytics, RFM segmentation and Power BI dashboards on more than a million Amazon India orders (2015–2025). This report summarises the key business insights and recommendations discovered during the project.

Domain: E-commerce / Retail Analytics
Tech: Python · Pandas · SQLite · SQL · Power BI
Data Volume: 1.12M+ transactions · 11 years
Focus: Trends · Customer behaviour · Product performance
01

Project Overview

The goal of this project is to understand how Amazon India’s sales evolved over a decade, which regions and categories drive revenue, and how customer segments behave in terms of recency, frequency and monetary value.

The dataset contains order-level information from 2015 to 2025 including prices, discounts, delivery details, payment methods, customer demographics, product ratings and return status. After cleaning and standardisation, the final analytics pipeline is built as:

  • Python + Pandas for data cleaning, feature engineering and combining yearly files.
  • SQLite + SQL for structured storage, performance optimisation and analytical queries.
  • RFM segmentation to profile customer value and behaviour.
  • Power BI for interactive dashboards to visualise revenue, orders and customer insights.

02

Data Preparation & Quality Checks

Consistent data quality was critical because the source contained 11 separate yearly CSVs with mixed formats, inconsistent categories, and noisy text fields.

Key cleaning steps (Python):

  • Standardised dates to ISO format and derived order_year, order_month and order_quarter.
  • Converted prices to numeric, handling commas, currency symbols and invalid entries.
  • Normalised boolean flags such as is_prime_member and is_festival_sale.
  • Re-mapped categories to a clean hierarchy (Electronics / subcategories like Smartphones, Laptops, Audio etc.).
  • Cleaned delivery_days, removing unrealistic values and coercing to numeric.
  • Removed duplicate transaction_id values and handled minor outliers in prices.

SQL modelling & indexing:

  • Loaded the cleaned dataset into an orders_cleaned fact table in SQLite.
  • Created helper dimension tables: customers, products, and time_dim for easier BI modelling.
  • Added indexes on order_year, order_month, customer_state, customer_city, payment_method, and transaction_id to speed up GROUP BY queries.

03

Sales Trends Over Time

Revenue and orders were analysed by year, quarter and month to find the growth pattern and peak years.

Yearly trend (2015–2025):

  • Orders grew from around 33K in 2015 to a peak of 143K in 2020.
  • Total revenue followed a similar pattern, peaking around 2020–2021 before softening slightly in later years.
  • Average order value increased strongly during the Covid period (2020–2021) and then normalised.

Seasonality (month & quarter):

  • Revenue spikes strongly in Q4 (Oct–Dec) across almost all years, driven by festival sales and year-end promotions.
  • Secondary peaks appear around January and August due to Republic Day / Independence Day events.
  • Q2 is usually the softest quarter in terms of revenue.
Insight: Amazon India saw its strongest growth between 2017–2020 with a clear seasonal peak in Q4 every year. Any future campaign planning should prioritise this quarter with aggressive marketing for high-value categories.
04

Customer Behaviour & RFM Segmentation

Customers were analysed by Prime membership, location tiers, age groups and RFM scores to understand spending behaviour.

Prime vs Non-Prime:

  • Prime members place fewer orders than non-Prime overall, but their average order value is significantly higher.
  • Prime customers show higher adoption of UPI / Card and faster delivery preferences.

Customer tiers & demographics:

  • Metro cities contribute the highest revenue, followed by Tier-1 cities.
  • Age groups 18–35 dominate order volume and revenue, consistent with a young, tech-savvy base.
  • Rural customers have lower AOV but present long-term expansion potential.

Top revenue states

  • Maharashtra, Delhi, Tamil Nadu and Karnataka lead the state-wise revenue ranking.
  • These states also show strong penetration of Prime membership and faster delivery.

Top revenue cities

  • Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore and Chennai are the top revenue-generating cities.
  • Smaller cities like Pune, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad and Surat also contribute significantly.

RFM Segmentation (SQL + Python):

  • Calculated Recency (days since last order), Frequency (number of orders) and Monetary (total spend per customer).
  • Assigned R, F, M scores and combined them to an RFM score with labels such as "Champions", "Loyal Customers", "Big Spenders", "At Risk" and "New / Inactive".

High-value segments

  • Champions: recent, frequent and high-spend customers.
  • Loyal customers: frequent buyers with good monetary value.
  • Big spenders: high monetary value but not necessarily frequent.

Risk / opportunity segments

  • At risk: high historical value but have not ordered recently.
  • New / inactive: recent but low frequency; need onboarding nudges.
Recommendation: Run targeted campaigns for each segment – reward Champions with exclusive launches, re-engage At Risk customers with discounts, and nurture New / Inactive users via personalised recommendations and emails.
05

Product, Category & Brand Performance

Sales were dominated by Electronics, but there were meaningful differences across subcategories and brands in terms of revenue and return rate.

Categories & subcategories:

  • The cleaned dataset shows that Electronics contributes almost the entire revenue (smartphones, laptops, audio, TVs, smart watches, tablets).
  • Within Electronics, Smartphones are the biggest driver, followed by Laptops, Audio, Smart Watches and TVs.

Top brands by revenue:

  • Samsung, Apple and OnePlus are the top three revenue-generating brands.
  • Other strong performers include Xiaomi, Realme, Vivo, Oppo, Lenovo and premium gaming brands like Alienware.

Rating patterns

  • Most products have 4.0+ product rating, indicating overall good product quality.
  • Customer ratings are slightly higher during festival periods, likely due to discounts increasing perceived value.

High-return categories

  • Subcategories such as Audio, Smart Watches, Tablets and Smartphones show higher-than-average return rates.
  • Returns are often linked to expectation mismatch (colour, sound quality, features) or technical issues.
Recommendation: For high-return categories, improve product detail pages (clear specs, comparison tables, authentic photos) and tighten quality checks for specific brands or models with repeat issues.
06

Festival Sales, Payment Methods & Delivery

Special festivals, payment choice and delivery speed all influence how and when customers place orders.

Festival vs non-festival:

  • Festival periods (Diwali, Great Indian Festival, Republic Day, etc.) generate a significant uplift in revenue and order volume.
  • Average order value during major festival events is typically higher due to bulk purchases and premium categories.

Payment trends (2015–2025):

  • Early years show heavy reliance on Cash on Delivery (COD).
  • Over time, **UPI and Card** payments grow rapidly and become the dominant modes from 2019 onwards.
  • Wallet and “Other” methods contribute smaller but growing shares.

Delivery performance

  • Average delivery time is around 3–4 days across India.
  • Some states (e.g., Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra) show slightly faster average delivery days than others.

Delivery & returns

  • There is only a weak correlation between delivery_days and return_rate, but extreme delays can still increase dissatisfaction.
  • Most returns fall within normal delivery times, suggesting product-related issues rather than logistics.
Operational note: continuing to shift customers from COD to UPI/Card reduces logistics risk and improves cash flow. Faster delivery SLAs in high-value metros can further differentiate the customer experience.
07

Ratings, Returns & Quality Insights

Returns are a key cost driver in electronics. We analysed return rate by category, brand, rating band and year.

Overall return rate (all years):

  • The global return rate is around 7% of orders, relatively stable across years.
  • Returns are slightly higher in some recent years, but differences are small.

By category, subcategory & brand:

  • Electronics overall: ~7% return rate.
  • Highest return subcategories: Audio, Smart Watches, Tablets, Smartphones, Laptops.
  • Some brands such as iQOO, Sennheiser, Boat, Fitbit, Audio-Technica show higher-than-average return rates and require closer monitoring.

Rating vs returns:

  • Orders with 4–5★ ratings have the lowest return rate.
  • Lower rating bands (2–3★) correlate with higher returns, as expected.
Actionable risk: Prioritise quality checks and customer feedback analysis for high-return brands and subcategories (especially Audio and Smart Watches). Negotiate with suppliers or adjust catalogue where return rates are persistently above category benchmark.

09

Key Business Takeaways & Next Steps

This project converts raw transaction data into a full analytics stack that can be reused and extended.

Summary of insights:

  • Strong growth from 2017–2020 with a clear revenue peak in 2020 and consistent Q4 seasonality.
  • Metro and Tier-1 cities, especially Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore and Chennai, drive the majority of revenue.
  • Electronics – especially smartphones and laptops – dominate sales but also generate higher-than-average returns in certain subcategories.
  • Shift from COD to UPI/Card improves payment efficiency; Prime members deliver higher AOV and prefer faster delivery.
  • RFM segmentation identifies Champions, Loyal, At Risk and New customers, enabling targeted campaigns.

Recommended next steps:

  • Integrate marketing data (ad spend, campaigns) with this pipeline to measure ROI by channel and segment.
  • Deploy automated dashboards with scheduled refreshes using the SQLite / SQL layer as a data mart.
  • Apply predictive models (churn prediction, propensity to buy) on top of the RFM-labelled customers.
  • Set up monitoring for high-return brands and ensure corrective actions with suppliers.
Final note: The combination of Python data cleaning, SQL analytics, RFM segmentation and Power BI dashboards creates a reusable blueprint for future e-commerce analytics projects beyond Amazon India.
